# HOW TO USE THE BRAND PLAYBOOK BLUEPRINT
### A simple guide to creating your brand playbook with AI

---

## WHAT YOU'LL GET

A professional 17-page brand playbook covering everything from your brand story and values to your logo system, color palette, typography, packaging, and messaging.

## WHAT YOU'LL NEED

1. **The blueprint file** (blueprint.md)
2. **Claude** (claude.ai) or **ChatGPT** (chatgpt.com) - free tiers work
3. **ChatGPT with image generation** (for creating the visual pages)
4. About **30-60 minutes** of your time

## BEFORE YOU START

Have these ready (even rough ideas are fine, the AI will help you refine):

- Your brand name and tagline
- Your brand colors (HEX codes if you have them, or just describe them)
- A sense of your brand's personality (e.g. "bold and playful" or "calm and premium")
- Who your audience is
- 2-3 brands whose look you admire

Don't worry if you don't have everything figured out. The blueprint will walk you through it.

---

## STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

### STEP 1: Upload the blueprint to Claude

Go to claude.ai, start a new conversation, and upload the `blueprint.md` file. Then type something like:

> "I uploaded my brand playbook blueprint. I want to create a brand playbook for my company. Can you help me fill in the details?"

Claude will read the file and start asking you questions about your brand. Answer them one by one. It will ask about your brand name, colors, fonts, personality, and so on.

If you already know your details, you can paste everything at once:

> "Here are my brand details:
> Brand name: [name]
> Tagline: [tagline]
> Industry: [what you do]
> Colors: [list your colors with HEX codes]
> ..."

### STEP 2: Get your 17 prompts

Once Claude has your brand details, it will generate 17 custom prompts. Each prompt is designed to create one page of your brand playbook.

Claude will also help you write the strategic content for pages that need written copy (your brand story, mission, values, etc.).

### STEP 3: Generate the visuals in ChatGPT

Open ChatGPT (you need the version with image generation).

For each of the 17 prompts:
1. Open a new ChatGPT conversation
2. Paste the prompt
3. Hit enter
4. Save the generated image

**Pro tip:** You can open multiple ChatGPT tabs and run several prompts at the same time. Each prompt is fully self-contained, so they don't need to be run in order.

### STEP 4: Assemble your playbook

You now have:
- 17 generated images (the visual pages)
- Strategic content for each page (the written copy)

Combine them in one of these ways:

**Option A: PowerPoint/Google Slides**
- Create a new presentation (16:9 aspect ratio)
- Set each generated image as a slide background
- Overlay the strategic text where needed

**Option B: Canva**
- Create a 16:9 presentation
- Upload each image as a page
- Add text overlays

**Option C: PDF**
- Import images into any PDF tool
- Add text layers
- Export as a single PDF

## THE 17 PAGES (WHAT'S IN YOUR PLAYBOOK)

| # | Page | What it covers |
|---|------|---------------|
| 01 | Cover | Brand name, tagline, hero visual |
| 02 | Brand Story | Why your brand exists, the problem you solve |
| 03 | Mission / Vision / Values | Your purpose, direction, and principles |
| 04 | Brand Archetype | Your brand's personality type (Sage, Hero, Creator, etc.) |
| 05 | Target Audience | Who you serve, with detailed personas |
| 06 | Brand Positioning | How you're different from competitors |
| 07 | Voice and Tone | How your brand speaks and writes |
| 08 | Logo System | Your logo, variations, spacing rules, misuse examples |
| 09 | Color System | Your full color palette with codes and usage rules |
| 10 | Typography | Your fonts, hierarchy, and pairing rules |
| 11 | Imagery & Art Direction | Photography style, visual mood, do's and don'ts |
| 12 | Signature Design Device | Your brand's unique visual element (like Nike's swoosh usage) |
| 13 | Packaging & Product | How your brand looks on products and interfaces |
| 14 | Brand Applications | Mockups: business cards, social media, website, merch |
| 15 | Partnership & Co-branding | Rules for when your logo appears next to others |
| 16 | Brand Do's & Don'ts | Visual examples of correct vs. incorrect usage |
| 17 | Messaging Cheat Sheet | Tagline, elevator pitch, key messages, brand promise |

## TIPS FOR BETTER RESULTS

**Be specific with colors.** "Blue" gives generic results. "#6366F1 Electric Indigo" gives precise results.

**Name real brands as references.** "Make it look premium" is vague. "Think Apple product photography meets Stripe's documentation" gives the AI a clear target.

**The avoid list matters.** Tell the AI what you DON'T want. "No generic AI gradients, no glow effects, no stock imagery" prevents the most common AI pitfalls.

**Run prompts in parallel.** Each prompt works independently. Open multiple tabs, paste one per tab, generate simultaneously.

**Iterate.** If a page doesn't look right, tweak the prompt and regenerate. Adjust the mood stack, style references, or layout instructions.

## FAQ

**Do I need to be a designer?**
No. The blueprint handles the design direction. You just need to know your brand.

**Can I use this for a client?**
Yes. Fill in their brand details instead of yours.

**What if I don't have brand colors yet?**
Tell Claude "I don't have colors yet" and describe the vibe you want. It will suggest a palette with HEX codes.

**What if I don't know my archetype?**
The blueprint includes a table of all 12 archetypes with example brands. Claude will help you pick the right one.

**Can I add more pages?**
Absolutely. The 17-page structure is a proven framework, but you can add pages for things like motion guidelines, social media templates, or editorial style.

**What's the difference between this and a one-shot AI brand kit?**
A one-shot brand kit gives you 6-8 panels of visual identity (logo, colors, type, mockups). This blueprint gives you 17 pages including brand strategy, positioning, voice, personas, and governance. The strategy layer is what makes a brand playbook actually useful.
